Salted Caramel Squares

Salted Caramel Squares are a scrumptious treat that combines the sweetness of caramel with a hint of salt to create a delightful dessert. These squares start with a buttery, crumbly crust on the bottom, providing a crunchy foundation. On top of this, a rich and gooey layer of caramel is poured, adding a sweet and sticky touch. The caramel is sprinkled with just a pinch of salt, which enhances the flavors and balances the sweetness. Finally, a layer of chocolate is drizzled over the top, adding a delicious finish. These squares are the perfect combination of sweet and salty, making them a mouthwatering delight. They are ideal for satisfying your sweet tooth, and the different layers of taste and texture make each bite a wonderful experience. Whether you're sharing them with friends or enjoying them on your own, salted caramel squares are a delightful dessert that is sure to please your taste buds.

Ingredients

Pastry Shell

  1. 1 stick unsalted butter, softened
  2. 1 cup confectioners' sugar
  3. 2 large eggs, beaten
  4. 1 teaspoon kosher salt
  5. 1 large egg white, beaten

Caramel

  1. 2 1/4 cups heavy cream
  2. 1 vanilla bean, split, seeds scraped
  3. 2 1/4 cups sugar
  4. 3/4 sticks unsalted butter
  5. 2 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt

Directions

Preheat the oven to 350°. Line a 9-by-13-inch baking pan with parchment paper, leaving a 2-inch overhang on the short sides. In a large bowl, using a handheld mixer at low speed, cream the butter. Beat in the confectioners' sugar. Add the whole eggs and beat until incorporated, then beat in the flour and salt. Press the pastry into the prepared pan in an even layer, 1/4 inch thick. Freeze until firm, 10 minutes.

Line the pastry with parchment paper and fill with pie weights. Bake for 35 minutes, until just set. Carefully remove the pie weights and parchment. Brush the shell with the egg white and bake for 20 minutes longer, until golden and cooked through. Let cool.

In a saucepan, bring the cream, vanilla bean and seeds to a simmer. Cover; keep warm. 4. In a large, heavy saucepan, stir the sugar into 1/4 cup of water. Simmer over moderate heat, without stirring, until a deep amber caramel forms, 7 minutes.

Remove the caramel from the heat and carefully add the cream. When the bubbling subsides, stir in the butter. Insert a candy thermometer and cook over moderately high heat, stirring with a wooden spoon, until the caramel reaches 240°, 10 minutes. Discard the vanilla bean and stir in the salt. Pour the caramel over the shell. Refrigerate until firm, 4 hours or overnight; bring to room temperature. Remove the bar from the pan using the parchment overhang; cut into squares.
